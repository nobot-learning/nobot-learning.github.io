NoBot Learning

CoRL 2025 Workshop on Robot Learning Without Robots

Submit a Paper
Robots need massive data to be useful
— but what if there's not enough robots in the world to collect it?

NoBot Learning confronts a fundamental challenge in robotics: the chicken-and-egg problem of robot intelligence. To make robots smarter, we need massive datasets collected by robots—but collecting such data requires already-smart robots deployed in the real world! This workshop aims to explore the possibility of a paradigm shift: instead of waiting for robot hardware to proliferate, we explore how to make robots smarter without any physical robots. Through human demonstrations, AR/VR, and synthetic data, we aim to create internet-scale datasets that could catalyze the development of generalist robot foundation models.

Topics of Interest

  • Using human videos and motions to train robots
  • AR/VR for robot-free data collection
  • Sim-native learning with sim-to-real transfer
  • Specialized robot-free data collection hardware systems
  • Training with synthetic and crowd-sourced data
  • Open benchmarks for robot-free learning
